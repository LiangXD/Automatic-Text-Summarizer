U.S. Tells China Taiwan Remarks a Mistake - Xinhua BEIJING -- The United States told China U.S. President George W. Bush had made a mistake when he called Taiwan a country in a speech last week, the official Xinhua news agency reported on Sunday. Reports in Taiwanese newspapers on Friday said Bush called the island the Republic of Taiwan and a country in a speech to diplomats and business executives on Thursday. The English-language ***Taipei Times ***newspaper quoted Bush as saying it was important to recognize and to welcome both countries, both the Republic of Taiwan, and of course China, into the World Trade Organization.
