HARARE: Robert Mugabe has won Zimbabwe's presidential election, Registrar General Tobaiwa Mudede announced on Wednesday. Mudede said on state television that Mugabe was re-elected with 1,685,212 votes against 1,258,758 votes for opposition challenger Morgan Tsvangirai. Results broadcast on state television gave Mugabe 1,634,382 votes, or 52 per cent of the 3.1 million ballots cast. His main rival Morgan Tsvangirai of the Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) had 1,185,890 votes, or 38 per cent of the votes cast. We have a national executive meeting... where we will be looking at the figures and give the reaction of the party, said David Coltart, MP for the Movement for Democratic Change (Mdc).
