US human rights report termed inaccurate ISLAMABAD, March 5: The government on Tuesday rejected the US State Department Report on human rights as "factually inaccurate and unwarranted" adding that it reflected tendency of some states to sit in judgment on others."The reports fails to acknowledge the important policy measures and initiatives by the government of Pakistan to promote and protect human rights," said a statement issued by Foreign Office spokesman in which the report was termed as factually inaccurate and unwarranted.Despite these developments, the report states "the government's human rights record remained poor."The government of Pakistan therefore, feels constrained to reject the assessment of the report about Pakistan, and the tendency of some states to sit in judgment over others."
