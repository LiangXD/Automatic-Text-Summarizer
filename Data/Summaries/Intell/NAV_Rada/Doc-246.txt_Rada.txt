Marshall Schwarz was named chairman and chief executive officer of U.S. Trust Corp., a private-banking firm with assets under management of about $17 billion.Mr. Schwarz, who is president of U.S. Trust will be succeeded in that post by Jeffrey S. Maurer, 42, who is executive vice president in charge of the company's asset-management group.Donald M. Roberts, 54, treasurer, and Frederick S. Wonham, 58, who takes responsibility for the funds-service group, were named vice chairmen and will serve in the office of the chairman.U.S. Trust a 136-year-old institution that is one of the earliest high-net worth banks in the U.S., has faced intensifying competition from other firms that have established, and heavily promoted, private-banking businesses of their own.The new services allow U.S. Trust to cater to the "new wealth," Mr. Schwarz says.Mr. Schwarz's father was a U.S. Trust trustee until 1974.Frederick B. Taylor, 48, also was named a vice chairman and chief investment officer, a new post.But Mr. Schwarz welcomes the competition in U.S. Trust's flagship businesses, calling it "flattery."
