Urumqi, January 21 (XINHUA) -- The Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region in northwest China will invest 420 billion yuan (51 billion U.S. dollars) in fixed assets during the country's 10th Five-Year Plan period for 2001-2005, compared with 254 billion yuan in the 1996-2000 period. Wang Lequan, secretary of the regional committee of the Communist Party of China, has listed comprehensive treatment of the Tarim River and the project to ship natural gas from Xinjiang to eastern China as the most important projects for this year. In 1996-2000, Xinjiang completed a big number of major transportation, energy, water conservancy and telecommunications projects with 254 billion yuan of fixed asset investment.
