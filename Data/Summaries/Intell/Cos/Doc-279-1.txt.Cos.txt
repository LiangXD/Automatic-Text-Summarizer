The St. Louis company earned $45.2 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with $84.9 million, or $1.24 a share, a year earlier. Sales in the latest period were $1.76 billion, a 13% increase from last year's $1.55 billion. Sales for the full year were $6.6 billion, up 13% from $5.8 billion. The battery plant, which makes rechargeable nickel cadmium and carbon zinc products, will be closed over the next year or so, a spokesman said. For the year, pet food volume was flat, the company said.
