The experts and scholars will be divided into discussion panels that will address the significance of Taiwan's elections and its democratic consolidation, a comparative analysis of democratic elections and Taiwan's democracy in the Asia Pacific region. According to the Government Information Office (GIO), which is co-sponsoring the symposium with National Chengchi University's Institute of International Relations, the symposium is being held to provide the world with a greater understanding of Taiwan's role in regional stability. Some of the visiting experts from 10 countries, including the United States, Canada, Britain, Germany, France and Japan, have been arriving since Wednesday to call on key government agencies and local political leaders ahead of Saturday's elections for legislators, county chiefs and city mayors.
