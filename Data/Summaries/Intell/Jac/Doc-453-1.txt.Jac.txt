Italian returns from Russian space mission Text of report in English by Italian news agency ANSA web site Moscow, 5 May: Italian astronaut Roberto Vittori, a Russian cosmonaut and a paying space tourist, returned to earth today from their ten-day mission, including eight days aboard the International Space Station (ISS). Shuttleworth, 28, who paid the Russians 20m dollars for his jaunt in space, termed the experience extraordinary. Vittori, 37, spent eight months at Russia's Star City in preparation for his 10-day mission, dubbed Marco Polo by the Italian Space Agency. During his 8 days aboard the ISS he carried out a series of experiments for ISA [as received] and the European Space Agency on health in space Vittori, a colonel and test-pilot with a wife and two small boys, already has three years training at NASA centres in Houston.
