Weaker imports shrink U.S. June trade gap. Glenn Somerville WASHINGTON 1996-08-20 Lower imports shrank the U.S. trade deficit dramatically in June , the Commerce Department said Tuesday , bolstering the impression of gradual waning in the economy's vigour. In June , the merchandise deficit fell 13.9 percent to $14.46 billion from $16.79 billion in May. The surplus on services climbed 1.6 percent to $6.34 billion from $6.25 billion in May.
