Lawsuits involving the Bard Avaulta mesh implant have been consolidated in federal court in the Southern District of West Virginia. The agency, however, appears unwilling to formally revoke approval of the mesh products, including Bard Avaulta mesh, for that indication. In theory, the product's use for women's unique health issues seemed to make sense. In practice, TVM mesh products—Bard mesh among them—have presented a myriad of health issues for women. The sensation of pulling within the abdomen while walking or bending has created mobility issues for some patients. For these patients, not only was the incontinence problem not resolved, the patient is made to live with serious adverse reactions that for some have led to depression, separation and divorce.
