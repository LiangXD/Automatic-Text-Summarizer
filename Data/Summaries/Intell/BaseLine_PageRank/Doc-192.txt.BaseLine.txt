Urumqi, January 21 (XINHUA) -- The Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region in northwest China will invest 420 billion yuan (51 billion U.S. dollars) in fixed assets during the country's 10th Five-Year Plan period for 2001-2005, compared with 254 billion yuan in the 1996-2000 period. Nearly 60 percent of the investment will come from the central government and state-owned enterprises, a local official said Monday. The money will mainly be used for the construction of 27 key projects in the fields of water conservancy, energy, transportation and environmental protection.
