Japan, U.S. Differ Over Global Warming Issues Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and visiting U.S. President George W. Bush differed over the Kyoto Protocol and how to prevent global warming but agreed to cooperate on that issue. Bush repeated his rejection of the 1997 Kyoto Protocol aimed atcurbing global warming, and pushed for the alternative he announced last week. Both the economy and the environment can be improved together.We'd like to see further efforts on the part of the U.S., the Japanese leader added.
