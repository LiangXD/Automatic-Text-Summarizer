the finnish government and major creditors of bankrupt shipyard waertsilae marine industries oy agreed in principle to form a new company to complete most of the troubled shipyard's backlog of 15 ships. the situation is that the bankruptcy court will get out of the shipbuilding business. everything will be taken over by the new company, said christian andersson, executive vice president of oy waertsilae, former parent of waertsilae marine. under an accord signed yesterday, the government and union bank of finland would become major shareholders in the new company, each injecting 100 million finnish markkaa ($23.5 million). oy waertsilae is to contribute 200 million markkaa, most of it as subordinated debt, and take a minority stake in the new company. carnival, which has three ships on order from waertsilae marine, presented claims for $1.5 billion damages in the bankruptcy court this week. waertsilae marine's bankruptcy proceedings began tuesday in a helsinki court.
