shuttleworth-mbeki by mariette le roux bloemfontein april 27 sapa mbeki speaks to 'afronaut' in outer space contact was made on saturday between outer space and the free state city of bloemfontein when president thabo mbeki spoke to africa's first astronaut mark shuttleworth, aboard the international space station. shuttleworth described his journey so far as very exciting, saying the flight had been smooth and everything was going according to plan. i cannot imagine anything as beautiful as that. he said he had seen the most magnificent sunset and sunrise over africa, at which mbeki asked him to bring pictures so that we can know what we look like to you from up there. the president then toasted shuttleworth with a glass of champagne, saying: i am sure this will go down in our history as one of those moments where we made quite a leap forward. shuttleworth, accompanied by russian flight commander yury gidzenko and italian cosmonaut roberto vittori, reached the iss at 0755 gmt, two days after blasting off from the baikonur cosmodrome in kazakhstan. the world's second space tourist, shuttleworth was paying 20 million dollars for his 10-day journey into space, where he would carry out experiments into the hi virus which causes aids.
