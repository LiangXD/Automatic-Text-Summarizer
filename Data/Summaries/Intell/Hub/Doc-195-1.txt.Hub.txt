LONDON, Jan 18 (AFP) -- British officials have arrived at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba and are assessing the conditions of three British prisoners among the 110 suspected al-Qa'ida fighters held there by the US, the Foreign Office said on Friday. A Foreign Office spokesman said: I can confirm a team of British officials are in the process of visiting the detainees at Guantanamo Bay. A spokesman for Prime Minister Tony Blair refused to say how many British officials had travelled to the camp.
