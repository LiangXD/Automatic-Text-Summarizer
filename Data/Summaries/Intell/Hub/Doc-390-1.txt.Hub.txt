U.S. Tells China Taiwan Remarks a Mistake - Xinhua BEIJING -- The United States told China U.S. President George W. Bush had made a mistake when he called Taiwan a country in a speech last week, the official Xinhua news agency reported on Sunday. Bush was referring to the entry late last year and this year of China and Taiwan, respectively, into the World Trade Organization, reports said. The English-language ***Taipei Times ***newspaper quoted Bush as saying it was important to recognize and to welcome both countries, both the Republic of Taiwan, and of course China, into the World Trade Organization.
