westinghouse electric corp., capitalizing on a major restructuring program, expects operating margins of more than 10% and double-digit per-share earnings growth next year, top officers told securities analysts here. john c. marous, chairman and chief executive officer, also said the company expects sales from continuing businesses to rise 8.5% annually through the next three years. since 1983, westinghouse has shed 70 businesses that it didn't expect to produce 10% operating margins while acquiring 55 businesses. westinghouse expects to meet its corporate goals despite a softening in the economy. a bright spot is the company's power-generation business, which is experiencing a surge of growth for the first time in years. mr. marous said the business will achieve higher sales this year than the company's target goal of 8.5%. westinghouse also expects its international sales to soon grow to 25% of total corporate sales from 20% last year.
