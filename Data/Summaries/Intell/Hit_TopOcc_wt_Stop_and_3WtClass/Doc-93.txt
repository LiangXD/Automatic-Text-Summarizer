new york 1997-03-31 coram healthcare corp stock fell monday after the company said its merger partner integrated health services inc lowered the amount it will pay for coram , traders said. the companies said the share-exchange ratio in their five-month-old agreement was amended to 0.15 integrated common shares for each coram common share from 0.2111 shares. integrated health said monday that based on the closing price of its stock on the last business day before execution of the new terms , it would be paying $4.35 per coram share. when the merger was first announced last october , it said coram holders would get about $5.50 worth of integrated stock for each coram share under the previous , fixed ratio. a coram spokesman said the new terms were negotiated in response to the _ overall pressure on the iv ( intravenous ) therapy industry. coram has been given more certainty of the merger closing by virtue of the $25 million breakup fee _ contained in the new terms that go into effect this friday. i still like the deal. _ coram also announced this morning a fourth quarter loss of $0.75 per share , or $31.6 million , including a $15.1 million provision for settlement of a shareholder lawsuit , versus a year-ago loss of $0.45 per share , or $17.6 million.
