italian returns from russian space mission text of report in english by italian news agency ansa web site moscow, 5 may: italian astronaut roberto vittori, a russian cosmonaut and a paying space tourist, returned to earth today from their ten-day mission, including eight days aboard the international space station (iss). i'm ready to go back, vittori told italian general mario arpino during a telephone call shortly after the russian soyuz space capsule made its touch-down in kazakhstan. russian officials said the three men, including the world's second space tourist south african millionaire mark shuttleworth, were in fine condition. vittori, 37, spent eight months at russia's star city in preparation for his 10-day mission, dubbed marco polo by the italian space agency. during his 8 days aboard the iss he carried out a series of experiments for isa [as received] and the european space agency on health in space vittori, a colonel and test-pilot with a wife and two small boys, already has three years training at nasa centres in houston. his training in russia combined with his knowledge of american techniques will put him at an advantage if the role of station commander should open up in the future, he said before his training started. this covered his training, that of a belgian substitute and the cost of carrying two scientific experiments on board the russian soyuz rocket.
