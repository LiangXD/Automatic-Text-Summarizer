President Reagan will nominate national security adviser Colin Powell for promotion to four-star rank and the post of commander-in-chief of the Army's Forces Command, the White House and Pentagon announced today."President Reagan has the highest respect and affection for General Powell and wishes him all success in his Army career," White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater said in announcing the nomination.The spokesman said that Powell is expected to assume command in April, 1989, when the current commander, Gen. Joseph T. Palastra Jr., retires.After leaving the White House Powell plans to visit a number of army units before assuming his command, Fitzwater said.In 1977 Powell was assigned to the Pentagon as an assistant to the deputy assistant secretary of defense.
