The spirit of a nation raced with Johnson when he won the gold medal in the 100 meters on Saturday with a world record time of 9.79 seconds.The stripping of Ben Johnson's Olympic gold medal in a drug scandal has wounded Canadian pride and shamed a nation hungry for a hero to replace hockey superstar Wayne Gretzky.Johnson's disgrace was heralded in Canadian headlines such as "Fool's Gold," "Black Day For Canada," "Seconds Of Glory, Years Of Shame," "From Fame To Shame," and "Big Ben is Now Has-Ben.""The Ben Johnson episode is a tragedy of shocking proportions.""He has left Canadians hanging their heads in shame," wrote the Corner Brook Western Star.He let Canada down," said Donny Clarke, 12.But columnist Gary Lautens of the Toronto Star said the nation should not cover its head in shame.
