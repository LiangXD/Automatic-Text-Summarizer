An earthquake is considered significant if it has a magnitude of 6.5 on the Richter scale.Although the annual average death toll for earthquakes is about 10,000, in 1987 only 1,100 lives were lost.6 an earthquake measuring 7.3 on the scale claimed 730 lives and injured about 4,000 people on the border between Burma and China, the Geological Survey reported.The estimated earthquake death toll in 1988 includes an additional 2,000 or more people who died in other earthquakes around the world, the Survey reported Monday.In addition to the deaths, some 13,000 people were injured and more than a half-million were left homeless in the Armenian earthquake, which had a magnitude of 6.8 on the Richter scale.The Survey said it recorded 61 significant earthquakes last year, 15 fewer than the year before.Earthquakes of lesser magnitude are included if they cause casualties or considerable damage.
