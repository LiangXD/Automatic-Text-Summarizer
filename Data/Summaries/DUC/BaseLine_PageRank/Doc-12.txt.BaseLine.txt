Government officials in Louisiana, Mississippi and Texas yesterday advised or ordered more than 2m people to evacuate coastal areas. The US industry's Property Claims Service, the official compiler of disaster losses, had yet to compile a preliminary tally of the Florida bill. Several forecasting agencies suggested the likeliest landfall was in central Louisiana, to the west of New Orleans, possibly late last night or this morning. Much of America's oil refining industry is concentrated along coastal Texas and Louisiana and several refineries were yesterday partially shut down. In Florida, Andrew caused greatest havoc in a largely suburban swathe some 10-15 miles south of Miami. More than 24 hours after the hurricane, some 825,000 households and businesses were still without power. A spokesman for State Farm Insurance said he believed the company had roughly 20 per cent of the Florida market. About 12 per cent of Travellers' home insurance premium income came from Florida last year, and 4.6 per cent of its commercial insurance premiums.
