Here is a chronology of the Iraq-Kuwait crisis: Aug. President Bush orders U.S. economic embargo against Iraq. 3 _ Shelling and gunfire echo through Kuwait City as Kuwaiti forces mount last-ditch resistance. 4 _ Iraq announces new military government for Iraqi-occupied Kuwait. European Community imposes trade embargo on Iraq. 5 _ Kuwait's new Iraqi-installed government claims Iraqi troops begin pulling out of Kuwait, but United States skeptical. 6 _ U.N. Security Council orders worldwide embargo on trade with Iraq. In Saudi Arabia, U.S. Defense Secretary Dick Cheney confers with Saudi leaders about defending the oil kingdom against any Iraqi attack. 7 _ Bush orders deployment of U.S. combat troops and warplanes to Saudi Arabia. Turkey cuts off exports from Iraqi oil pipeline through Turkish territory. Britain agrees to join multinational force in the gulf. Other oil-producing nations indicate they will step up production to make up for embargoed Iraqi and Kuwaiti oil. 9 _ American troops take up position in Saudi Arabia's oil-producing province. 10 _ Twelve of 20 Arab League states vote to send all-Arab military force to join Americans in defense of Saudi Arabia. 11 _ Saudi Arabian anti-aircraft batteries reportedly fire on Iraqi reconnaissance planes near Kuwaiti border. Thousands of Arabs demonstrate in Yemen and Jordan against United States. Secretary of State James A. Baker III says the U.S. Navy will interdict Iraqi oil shipments. 13 _ Iraqi troops in Kuwait round up American and British visitors from two hotels in Kuwait for transport to Iraq. 14 _ King Hussein flies to Washington in effort to mediate U.S.-Iraq confrontation. 21 _ Arab and Western nations condemn Saddam's threats against hostages and continue adding armed forces to the region.
