The statement from the Slovenian presidency followed Serbia's decision Tuesday to ban all political and economic contacts with Slovenia. Earlier Friday 13 people were arrested when police dispersed a crowd of about 100 gathered for a pro-Serbian rally in Ljubljana that had been prohibited by Slovenian authorities. In its statement, Slovenia's collective presidency called Serbia's action "a flagrant violation of all constitutional, legal and civilized norms." "We shall never permit anyone to drive us away or oust us from Yugoslavia," it said. Those who do not acknowledge this separate themselves from the federal state." Aleksandar Prlja, Serbia's secretary for foreign affairs, told The Associated Press that Slovenia's "banning of the peaceful rally was deeply humiliating for the Serbian people.
