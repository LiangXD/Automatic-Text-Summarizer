A 1987 state constitutional amendment broadening the right to bear arms means that even convicted felons may own guns, a judge ruled. "Maine's right to keep and bear arms amendment is the most broad and least restrictive of any of the 43 similar state amendments," he wrote. The Maine constitution used to guaranteed the right to keep and bear arms "for the common defense." The amendment declared, "Every citizen has a right to keep and bear arms,and this right shall never be questioned."
