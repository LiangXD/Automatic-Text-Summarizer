Third World countries led by Brazil, the world's most indebted developing nation, blamed the industrialized nations in part Monday for perpetuating their poverty.A similar theme sounded Monday in West Berlin, where finance ministers of 22 developed or developing countries demanded "more forceful action" to help Third World countries repay $1.2 trillion in debts.It is critical of the U.S. view that strong economic performance in developed countries would trickle down to help the Third World.
