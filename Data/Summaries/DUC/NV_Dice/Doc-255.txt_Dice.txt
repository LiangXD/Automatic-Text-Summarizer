The focus of the drought plaguing much of the nation has shifted eastward, but little relief is in sight for most areas, the National Weather Service reported Thursday.However, in the Midwest, the weather service reported that large areas have experienced the driest April-June period on record since 1895.Long-term drought conditions now cover a large area of the Great Lakes region and the Ohio and Tennessee valleys, the agency's Climate Analysis Center reported.Reservoir levels remain close to average in much of the nation, however, the weather service noted.
