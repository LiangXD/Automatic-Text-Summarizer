a major issue hanging over the 1990 census is resolved with the decision to include military personnel stationed overseas, but congress is arguing over whether to keep counting illegal aliens. census figures are used to redistribute the 435 house seats among the states every 10 years and to distribute federal aid to local governments. the reapportionment issue has riveted the attention of house members from states where slow growth threatens to result in losses of house seats. undocumented aliens are largely concentrated in the south, west and industrial states like new york, and other states fear a loss of power to those areas if the aliens are counted. that has yet to be decided, but possibilities include counting the state where the person owns a home or the last state where he or she lived for at least six months. tom ridge, r-pa., unsuccessfully sought to attach a ban on counting aliens to an appropriations measure providing $5.8 million to run the state, justice and commerce departments next year, including $800 million to take the census.
