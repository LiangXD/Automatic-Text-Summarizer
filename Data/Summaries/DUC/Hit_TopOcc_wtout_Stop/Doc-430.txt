lincoln savings &amp loan owner charles h. keating jr., his family and his business empire poured nearly $350,000 into state politics during 1985 and 1986, with about half of it going to gov. the figures do not include at least $83,336 in contributions made personally or through the firms of two other keating associates -- los angeles attorney karl m. samuelian and san diego businessman tom c. stickel. in april, keating's american continental corp. of phoenix filed for bankruptcy, prompting federal officials to immediately take over its subsidiary lincoln savings &amp loan. keating also paid $200,000 in 1985 for the consulting services of former savings and loan commissioner lawrence w. taggart, who had left government service in december, 1984, for a job with tcs enterprises of san diego. his coalition of contributors included his family -- son charles keating iii and three sons-in-law -- and his corporations, including american continental, lincoln savings and three other subsidiaries. while keating himself gave only $7,000 -- all of it to deukmejian -- the coalition of his relatives, business interests and associates brought total donations to $349,500 in 1985 and 1986. the governor received $80,000 directly from american continental and lincoln savings $19,000 from keating and his family members $51,000 from other american continental subsidiaries and executives and $23,000 from keating business associates.
