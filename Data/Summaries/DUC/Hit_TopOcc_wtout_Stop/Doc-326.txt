in response to questions about mr. sununu's recent shift to use of corporate jets in the wake of restrictions imposed last month on his use of government planes, the white house released the list of five jets the chief of staff has used for travel to fund-raisers. the white house list said that on may 20 mr. sununu used a corporate jet provided by donald bryant, a benefits and insurance executive, to travel to a st. louis fund-raiser for missouri gov. on june 7, mr. sununu used a jet provided by fiber materials inc. of biddeford, maine, to travel to a fund-raiser for the maine republican party. mr. sununu used a jet provided by three individual owners -- identified by the white house as howard bender, martin bender and john mason -- to travel to a chicago fund-raiser june 11. and, as also previously disclosed, mr. sununu used a jet provided by kvi, an iowa insurance brokerage firm, to travel tuesday to a fund-raiser in des moines. mr. sununu has resorted to regular use of corporate planes for political travel in the wake of controversy over his heavy use of costly government aircraft for political and personal travel in the past two years. but mr. bush, who spoke to reporters before a white house meeting as mr. sununu sat nearby, also seemed to suggest that he would like his chief of staff to avoid creating new travel controversies.
