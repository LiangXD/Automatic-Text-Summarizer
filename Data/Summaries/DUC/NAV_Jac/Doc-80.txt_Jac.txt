At the Pentagon, spokesman Pete Williams said the number of U.S. aircraft accidents in the gulf - including three separate crashes this week - were not out of the ordinary.Williams said the Air Force was the only service that has taken special measures to review safety in the gulf since this week's accidents.He said pilots of those planes received special safety briefings but their flight schedules were not changed.Also, 20 other aircraft have been involved in non-fatal accidents in the gulf area, Williams said.Williams said the Air Force gave its pilots in the gulf "safety awareness" briefings during the flight ban in order "to review what they need to do to fly more safely."
