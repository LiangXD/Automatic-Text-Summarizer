The court in Cruikshank said that the Second Amendment did not create a federally protected right of individuals but was an express limitation on the powers of the national (federal) government.The Second Amendment was, as was the rest of the first 10 amendments (the Bill of Rights), insisted upon by some of the states for their own protection from the federal government.1) A restriction on the power of the federal government to limit or deny the states the right to have state militia.There have been lower federal court decisions regarding the Second Amendment, all supportive of this established principle.
