the tornado touched down at 4:30 a.m. just west of madison, about 50 miles east of tallahassee, and cut a 12-mile swath of destruction, authorities said. fifteen people were injured, but none of the injuries was critical, according to madison county memorial hospital administrator jeannie baker. two of the deaths occurred in mobile homes blown apart by the tornado, and four other houses were seriously damaged as well, said madison county civil defense director bernard wilson. the tornado was part of a storm system that struck across the southeast with high winds, lightning and hail. two tornadoes struck near the towns of malone and bascom, about 100 miles to the west of madison, said jackson county emergency management director john mader. more than one inch of rain fell in one hour this morning in waycross, ga., 70 miles northeast of here, and wind gusts of 40 mph were reported in valdosta, according to the national weather service. in the eastern georgia's bulloch county, six mobile homes and one house were heavily damaged shortly after dawn today, but no injuries were reported, the weather service said.
