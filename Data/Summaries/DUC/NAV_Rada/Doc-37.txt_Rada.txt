Mexico, where sun worship has passed from ancient civilizations to modern beachgoers, is gearing up early for the day next year when the sun will be blotted out.The National University is developing six half-hour documentaries on eclipses and 10 TV spots on the dangers of looking at the sun, astronomy professor Jesus Galindo says.It will be the last chance to see a total solar eclipse this century.On July 11, the moon will pass between the planet and the sun, abruptly swallowing midday's light, except for a weird glow on the horizon.Since September, the university newspaper has been publishing installments of a book called "Eclipse" that will be translated into Indian languages.An eclipse is an astronomical coincidence.
