col. herminio taylo, 54, police chief of makati, had just finished jogging and was buying fruit in a public market when two assassins opened fire with.45-caliber pistols, police said. lydio zeta quoted witnesses as saying the gunmen shouted "we are npas," referring to the rebel new people's army, and warned bystanders not to interfere. the new people's army also claimed responsibility for the april 21 assassination of u.s. army col. james "nick" rowe, who was slain on his way to work at the joint u.s. military assistance group headquarters in suburban quezon city. gen. alexander aguirre, capital region commander, announced formation of a special task force to track down rebel assassins in the capital. president corazon aquino deplored the latest killing but said public officials must learn to live with the threat of assassination. "it is really very unfortunate and i do sympathize with the family of col. taylo," said mrs. aquino, whose husband benigno was slain in a political assassination in 1983. national security adviser rafael ileto said the killing of taylo showed that every soldier and policeman was a potential target of assassination.
