US INSURERS expect to pay out an estimated Dollars 7.3bn (Pounds 3.7bn) in Florida as a result of Hurricane Andrew - by far the costliest disaster the industry has ever faced.With Florida's Hurricane Andrew losses added in, the total rises to Dollars 11.2bn.Hurricane Andrew leaves the US property-casualty insurers facing their worst-ever year for catastrophe losses.This easily exceeds the record Dollars 7.6bn of catastrophe losses seen in 1989, when the industry paid out on both Hurricane Hugo and the Loma Prieta earthquake in California.
