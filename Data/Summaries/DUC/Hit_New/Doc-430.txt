lincoln savings &amp loan owner charles h. keating jr., his family and his business empire poured nearly $350,000 into state politics during 1985 and 1986, with about half of it going to gov. in april, keating's american continental corp. of phoenix filed for bankruptcy, prompting federal officials to immediately take over its subsidiary lincoln savings &amp loan. in california, keating hired key deukmejian supporters or appointees to help protect his ill-fated lincoln savings. keating also paid $200,000 in 1985 for the consulting services of former savings and loan commissioner lawrence w. taggart, who had left government service in december, 1984, for a job with tcs enterprises of san diego. his coalition of contributors included his family -- son charles keating iii and three sons-in-law -- and his corporations, including american continental, lincoln savings and three other subsidiaries. the governor received $80,000 directly from american continental and lincoln savings $19,000 from keating and his family members $51,000 from other american continental subsidiaries and executives and $23,000 from keating business associates. they also gave $17,000 to four legislators -- nolan, who authored the law deregulating the savings and loan industry in 1982 assembly rules committee chairman tom bane (d-van nuys) assemblyman bruce bronzan (d-fresno), and assemblyman dennis brown (r-los alamitos). * stickel and tcs contributed $50,666 in money, staff time and office space to deukmejian and the republican party.
